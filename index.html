Why This Community Thrives

The blueprint behind a comment section with almost zero negativity.

The niche is remarkably self-selecting. People seeking content about late-diagnosed ADHD/AuDHD typically arrive from a place of personal pain and relief — they're not there to argue, they're there because they finally feel understood.

But a self-selecting niche only hands you a calm room. Keeping it safe is active work. I set the tone in every caption, model the vulnerability I'm asking other people for, and reply personally so the space stays warm enough that someone will share a brand-new diagnosis in the comments. The culture is facilitated, not lucky.

The tone is non-confrontational and deeply empathetic, which attracts the same energy back. There are no hot takes or political flashpoints, so there's very little for bad-faith actors to latch onto. And where Instagram's filters did flag a handful of spam comments on sensitive topics, they were caught before they ever landed visibly — a sign of a healthy, well-moderated space.

The result is a community where members return post after post, share their own stories unprompted, and support one another — the hallmark of operations done right, not just content done well.
